1970 Dodge Challenger R/T

What we have here is a beautiful 1970 Dodge Challenger R/T 440 6-Pack, 4 Speed Convertible Tribute car with less than 100 miles on the odometer since its spectacular build. The car is finished in Mango with a complementary Black vinyl interior and top. This 1970 Dodge Challenger R/T 440 Tribute was spared no expense when restored. Rebuilt to the specs of the original (and there were only 61 cars built with this configuration), the cost to build this car exceeded $150,000. The paint is beautiful, the top is new as are the tires, and the interior is exceptional. The R/T 440 motor coupled to the manual 4-speed transmission was THE way to go back in the day; add a convertible top and the car was special.

1970 Dodge Challenger R/T

Incredible Pro-Touring build! Gen 3 SRT8 fuel injected Hemi, 4-speed with Gear Vendors overdrive, coil-over suspension, Wilwood discs, and more! Full custom interior. A complete and total show-stopper!

The stunning yellow paint is a bit more vivid than then usual Top Banana Yellow that Ma Mopar was using in 1970, and rendered in two-stage urethane, it simply glows. Every square inch of the body was stripped and refinished to show standards and the sheet metal is straight enough to calibrate NASA instrumentation. The doors open and close so easily that you forget this is a 45-year-old convertible and as you'd expect from a car at this level, the gaps are exacting. Sadly, from the driver's seat you won't be able to see the super-trick LED taillight display that uses sequential turn signals and brake lights for an awesome effect that's probably worth money to see.
